OpenAI was founded in December 2015 by Sam Altman, Elon Musk, Ilya Sutskever, Greg Brockman, Wojciech Zaremba, and John Schulman. The company is focused on developing friendly artificial intelligence. In 2018, Elon Musk resigned from the board of OpenAI due to potential conflicts of interest with his role as CEO of Tesla.

Tesla, Inc. was founded in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning. Elon Musk joined Tesla in 2004 as an investor and became its CEO in 2008. Tesla is headquartered in Austin, Texas. In 2016, Tesla acquired SolarCity, a solar energy services company.

SolarCity was founded in 2006 by Lyndon Rive and Peter Rive. The company was headquartered in San Mateo, California, before it was acquired by Tesla.

SpaceX was founded in 2002 by Elon Musk with the goal of reducing space transportation costs and enabling the colonization of Mars. SpaceX is headquartered in Hawthorne, California.

Apple Inc. was founded in April 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. Apple is headquartered in Cupertino, California. In 1997, Apple acquired NeXT, a computer company founded by Steve Jobs in 1985 after he left Apple.

NeXT developed NeXTSTEP, an object-oriented multitasking operating system. Following the acquisition of NeXT by Apple, the NeXTSTEP system was used as the foundation to develop Mac OS X (now macOS).

Google was founded in September 1998 by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University. Google is headquartered in Mountain View, California. Google is now a subsidiary of Alphabet Inc., which was created in 2015 as Google's parent company.

In 2005, Google acquired Android Inc., a mobile operating system startup founded by Andy Rubin. Android became the basis for the Android operating system, which Google launched in 2007.

In 2006, Google acquired YouTube, a video-sharing platform founded by Chad Hurley, Steve Chen, and Jawed Karim in 2005.

Microsoft Corporation was founded in April 1975 by Bill Gates and Paul Allen. Microsoft is headquartered in Redmond, Washington. Microsoft invested $13 billion in OpenAI between 2019 and 2024 to secure cloud infrastructure partnership and AI model licensing.

In 2018, Microsoft acquired GitHub, a software development platform, for $7.5 billion. GitHub was founded in 2008 by Tom Preston-Werner, Chris Wanstrath, P. J. Hyett, and Scott Chacon.

Meta Platforms, Inc., formerly named Facebook, was founded in February 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. Meta is headquartered in Menlo Park, California.

In 2012, Meta (then Facebook) acquired Instagram, a photo-sharing app founded by Kevin Systrom and Mike Krieger, for $1 billion.

In 2014, Meta acquired WhatsApp, a messaging application, for $19 billion. WhatsApp was founded in 2009 by Jan Koum and Brian Acton.
